Assessments are light and fun. At the same time, how you get along with the results changes how much insight you take away. Here are three mindsets for enjoying Grader in a healthy way.

## 1. A result is a hypothesis, not a verdict

A result doesn't pin you down. It's **a hypothesis — "maybe this is a tendency of mine."** If it rings true, take it; if it doesn't, let it go. That kind of distance feels just right.

## 2. The insight matters more than the score

More than the score or type itself, the value is in **the moment you think "now that you mention it, maybe so."** Use the result as a doorway to look back on your everyday self.

## 3. Try again, later

As your mood and life stage change, your results shift a little too.

> The you of six months from now might give a slightly different answer. Noticing that change is part of the fun.

Relax, and treat it as one mirror among many. Start with whichever assessment catches your eye.
