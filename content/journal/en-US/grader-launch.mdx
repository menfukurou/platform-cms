**Grader**, operated by Terumiu, is an open assessment platform anyone can try with ease. In this post, we'll give you a full tour of what Grader does and the experience that awaits.

## What's an assessment, anyway?

Put simply, it's a way to visualize your tendencies by answering a series of questions.

Personality, thinking habits, where your interests lean, the skills you're strong at — Grader gently sketches the things that are hard to see, from the answers you give. Rather than labeling you like a fortune teller, we aim for something you can use as a mirror for looking at yourself anew.

## Three steps to your results

Using it is wonderfully simple.

| Step | What happens |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Pick an assessment by category or difficulty |
| 2 | Answer anywhere from a few to a few dozen questions |
| 3 | Review your visualized score and explanation — sign in to save and share |

From quick ones that finish in a handful of questions to in-depth ones worth sitting with, choose based on your mood and your goal.

## Results, in a score and an explanation

The moment you finish, your results appear.

- **Overall score** — a single indicator that captures your tendency at a glance
- **Per-trait radar** — strengths and balance, visualized from many angles
- **Type and explanation** — your tendencies, put into words

> What matters isn't a high score, but the realization: "Ah, so this is how I tend to be." Your results are material for thinking about the next step.

## The same experience, in many languages

Grader supports Japanese, English, and more. When the language changes, the quality of the designed experience stays the same — so people across countries and regions can share the same assessments.

## Here's how you might use it

- When you want an objective view of your personality and strengths
- When you'd like a hint for where to learn or grow
- When you want to deepen mutual understanding in a team or community
